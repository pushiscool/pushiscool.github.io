Unit 1 – Thinking Geographically & Development

Introducing Argentina

Argentina or Argentine Republic lies in South America’s southern cone between the Andes and Atlantic. Major toponyms include Buenos Aires, Córdoba, Rosario, Mendoza, and Salta. Sites range from fertile Pampas to Andean peaks and sub‑Antarctic glaciers.

Divisions

Argentina has 23 provinces plus the autonomous city of Buenos Aires. Provinces group into Pampas, Northwest, Mesopotamia, Cuyo, and Patagonia regions.

Choropleth Map

Population‑density choropleth highlights dense Buenos Aires Province and sparsely populated Patagonia.

Cartogram

Provinces inflate with adolescent health coverage; Buenos Aires swells while poorer northern provinces shrink.

Proportional Symbol Map

Circle size marks adolescents meeting health benchmarks; largest circles cluster in Buenos Aires and Córdoba.

Dot Density Map

Dots mark service cohorts—dense near Rosario, sparse across Patagonia.

Environmental Determinism

Pampas grasslands foster cattle culture and leatherwear, Andean altiplano promotes adobe housing and quinoa dishes, while cold Patagonia favors wool garments.

Possibilism Example

Irrigation canals and railways transformed the Pampas into a grain powerhouse, illustrating human modification of landscape.

World Systems Theory

Wallerstein classifies Argentina as semi‑periphery: partly industrial, still commodity‑dependent.

Key Metrics

GDP $612 B, GNI $9 k, Gini 40.7, Literacy 99 %, HDI 0.865, Life expectancy 78.6 y.

Gender Inequality

GII 0.287 (rank 69). Women hold 43 % of parliamentary seats.

Rostow Stage

Argentina fits “Drive to Maturity”: diversified industries, expanding services, shrinking farm share.

Unit 2 – Population & Migration

Population & Density

Population 45 851 378 (2025). Arithmetic 17/km², Physiological 142/km², Agricultural 7 farmers/km² arable.

Vital Rates

CBR 10.7 ‰, CDR 8.6 ‰, NIR 2.1 ‰, IMR 8.4 ‰, TFR 1.36.

Population Pyramid

Slow‑growth pyramid reveals ageing society and stable workforce.

Demographic Transition

Stage 4: low births and deaths, long life expectancy.

Migration Profile

Immigrants 1.4 M, Emigrants 580 K, Net migration –0.08 ‰.

Push & Pull Factors

Push influences include persistent inflation, currency volatility, and limited skilled‑job creation. Pull factors involve higher wages, currency stability, and broader career paths in destinations such as Spain and the United States. Combined, they produce modest net out‑migration.

Unit 3 – Culture

Folk Culture

Empanadas, mate, bombachas trousers, tango dance, pato sport, and adobe estancias embody folk traditions.

Pop Culture

Football fandom around Boca Juniors and Lionel Messi demonstrates global pop influence.

Language

Spanish—Indo‑European family, Romance branch, Castilian group—diffused via colonial relocation; Rioplatense dialect emerged locally.

Religion

Roman Catholic 68 %, Evangelical 17 %, other 15 %.
